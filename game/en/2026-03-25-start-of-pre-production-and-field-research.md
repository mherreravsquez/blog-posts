## The Beginning
Two weeks ago, together with my classmate Antonia Benz, we started the Methodology course, which serves as a preliminary project for our final thesis. In this course, we will focus on handling all the pre-production work for our final thesis project, which in this case will be “Hunters: Vega” (WIP).
We wanted to develop something within the Hunters multiverse, so that it stops being just a single game (Hunters: Awakening) and start becoming a franchise in its own right, from which we can spin off different games in different genres—something similar to what the Metal Gear series does, with games like Metal Gear 4 or Metal Gear Rising, which don’t follow the main stealth-based storyline but instead lean more toward shooter or hack-and-slash genres, respectively.
Getting back to Hunters: Vega, this would be a game that follows the JRPG style with turn-based combat, but veers slightly toward the roguelike genre. We still have several ideas and elements in the works, which is why we don’t want to take anything for granted; however, what we are absolutely certain of is where we want to set the story of this sequel: La Vega Central, also known as La Vega, is a market located at the southern end of the Recoleta district in Santiago, almost reaching the north bank of the Mapocho River. Its stalls mainly sell fruits and vegetables sourced from the agricultural sectors of Chile’s Central Zone. This is a place full of “life” and with an unparalleled atmosphere, which we want to replicate in some way within this installment, supporting the traditional narrative with visual and auditory elements that form part of the ambient narrative.

## Field Research

As I mentioned earlier, our methodology began about 2–3 weeks ago, a period we’ve used to research mechanical, narrative, and historical references; but something was missing: research on the location where we wanted to set the game’s story. Although there are several photos and information about the market’s general history online, nothing compares to field research. That is why we decided to visit the Vega Central to map the area, take photographs, take notes, describe the place, and interview the various vendors; Although we were unable to interview many of the vendors because they were so busy, we were able to take several photos and describe the place in detail. Below is a brief summary of the research conducted:

### 1. Vega Central

#### 1.1 General Layout
La Vega Central is designed as a large warehouse with a highly complex spatial layout, organized into 14 main aisles and multiple unnumbered extensions. Notable features include:

- Presence of specific functional zones:
  - Loading and parking areas integrated into the aisles (especially in aisles 8 and 14).
  - Food court strategically located between aisles 10 and 12.
  - Expansion into adjacent areas such as the warehouse with the colorful roof.
- High interior verticality, with visible upper levels housing storage rooms, kitchens, and worker circulation.
- Heterogeneous infrastructure:
  - Roof with visible repairs.
  - Robust perimeter enclosure.
  - Multiple entrances of varying scales (main and secondary).
- Floor conditions:
  - Asphalt surface, often wet or dirty.
  - Color-coded signage in secondary aisles indicating hazards (e.g., slippery surfaces).

#### 1.2 Social and Sensory Dynamics
- Strong social cohesion among tenants, with rapid circulation of informal information.
- Disruptions to commercial activity occur only in the event of conflicts or security issues.
- Constant presence of:
  - Institutional security in peripheral areas.
  - Street vendors in inner sectors, inversely proportional to surveillance.
- Spatial stratification:
  - Inner sectors feature older, less modernized businesses.
  - Outer zones show greater commercial renewal.
- Sensory experience:
  - Predominance of smells specific to each category (vegetables, fruits, meats, fish, spices).
  - Soundscape dominated by voices, vendors’ calls, and machinery, with occasional music.
- Diversity of participants:
  - Coexistence of older workers, young migrants, and entire families.

### 1.3 Colorful Roofed Shed
- A more open transitional space, featuring a wide walkway covered by a multicolored roof.
- Direct connection to the main parking lot.
- Presence of narrow side alleys with low foot traffic.
- Commercial specialization:
  - Predominance of vegetables and products of Peruvian origin.
  - High level of background noise with a variety of simultaneous musical genres.

### 2. Vega Chica

#### 2.1 Market Layout
- An enclosed space, smaller in scale and more densely packed than the Vega Central.
- Organized into a few narrow aisles.
- Upper levels within the stalls.

#### 2.2 Market Dynamics
- Specialization in meat and seafood products.
- Limited availability of spices or complementary products.
- Strong ties to the informal external trade:
  - Street vending of food, cleaning products, and other supplies.
- Functional layout of the premises:
  - First level designated for consumption (dining areas).
  - Second level designated for preparation (kitchens).
  - Presence of hybrid formats (counter service or “takeout”).

#### 2.3 Adjacent Corridor
- A separate linear space that is functionally integrated.
- Distinct retail offerings:
  - Party supplies, stationery, and packaged confectionery.
  - Enhances the programmatic diversity of the complex.

### 3. Tirso Molina

#### 3.1 Structure
- A two-story building with a distinctive architectural design (roof shaped like inverted pyramids).
- Spacious, orderly, and well-ventilated interior.
- Modern infrastructure:
  - Escalators.
  - Elevator.

#### 3.2 Commercial Structure
- First level:
  - Sale of vegetables and cured meats.
- Second level:
  - Predominantly restaurants, especially those serving Peruvian cuisine and seafood.
  - A smaller presence of non-food retail (clothing, personal care products).

#### 3.3 Outdoor Environment
- High volume of street vendors, particularly those selling fried foods.
- Physical conditions of the environment characterized by greasy residue on the pavement.
